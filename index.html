UX Career Explorations

We are a group of UCLA graduate and undergraduate students broadly interested in the field of design. Sponsored by the UCLA Career Center, our main focus is the exploration of User Experience careers, especially as practiced by professionals with varying backgrounds.

Who We Are

Jonathan Calzada

Jonathan Calzada is a doctoral student in the Department of Information Studies. Jonathan’s research investigates how disadvantaged students approach higher education, and how information technologies inform their conceptualizations of the academic environment.

Takeaway: As the role of digital products expands in the consciousness of 21st century publics, business organizations are increasingly adopting a human-centered design approach to computer software production in an effort to innovate and reach a broader consumer base. However, producing for dominant institutions and mainstream consumers often excludes more people than it includes thereby rendering a vast range of them invisible.

Peter Polack

Peter Polack is a doctoral student in the Department of Information Studies studying how computational power structures are expressed through visual and interactive forms.

Takeaway: User Experience design is an absolutely heterogeneous field that sometimes verges on being an ethical orientation as opposed to a discipline or a methodology. With attention to the world, User Experience designers have the capability to experiment with new ways of bridging boundaries between people and marginalized communities.

What We Did

Our career exploration and inquiry adopted User Experience methods. As a group, we conducted participatory workshops and informational interviews. During our workshops we interviewed UX professionals, discussed relevant topics, and shared personal experiences in the field. The meetings culminated in data synthesis sessions and creation of this site to which we have posted our findings.

What We Learned

  • About the division of labor among User Experience professionals such as User Research and Design, as well as some of the implications within the production of technology or information as knowledge.
  • About resistance culture pertaining to a shift from predominating engineer-centric mindset to a design and research-oriented model. This included discussions about a UX Maturity Model.
  • About how standards shape the efficiency and affect production of software on the one hand, but may reduce agency and discourage creativity on the other.
  • About philosophies within the field of design such as those valuing semantics over syntax, theory over praxis, or those infusing curiosity into their ways of seeing, thinking, and doing, which culminated in the phrase “be curiosity” as an embodiment of that practice.
  • About accessibility in design which is to say the “User Experience of People with Disabilities” (Cordingley 2018). An overview of the different types of disabilities and considerations that are necessary in order to properly design for typically marginalized users. The presentation and discussions around this topic included methods and practices that are currently used in the field of software/technology development.
  • Advice on portfolio content and type of work for aspiring designers and researchers.
  • About specific work that UX professionals perform in the field:
    • UX Personas
    • Types of Usability Studies
    • User interviews and skills
    • App wireframe production
    • Data visualization skills
    • Sale skills as it pertains to stakeholder ‘buy-in’
    • The need for storytelling
    • The need for proper survey design
    • Considering ethics in all processes
    • Accessibility design
